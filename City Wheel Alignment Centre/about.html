    ... Process Of Wheel Refubrishment

    First step is cleaning. After removing tyre, clean the wheel with some warm water and washing up liquid. It will help to remove the dirt and contaminants.

    Next step is sanding. If you want to achieve a matte finish, then use sand paper 400 grit with plenty of water and detergent to prevent the paper from clogging up. Sand all the area for perfect finishing.

    After sanding, wash the wheel and dried off. When the wheel is completely dry you can then start filling the curbed marks with the help of body filler. Then allow the filler at least 24 hours before sanding. If the filler is completely dry you can start sanding. You may use 400 grit and plenty of water.

    Before spray filling, make sure the wheel is clean. If the wheel is clean you can start spraying the spray filler. The aim is to fill the small imperfections left from sanding the filler. You may spraying three coats. Leave about fifteen minutes between each coat. Then again sand, but before you do let the spray filler dry at least 24 hours.

    After the long winded preparation stages you can now finally get down to painting alloy wheels. Then spray the alloy wheel paint. Then the wheel is completely dry, you can apply primer on your wheel. You may spray three light coats, allowing fifteen minutes between each. If the primer is completely dry, sand again.

    Then apply color coat on your wheel. After this step polishing and waxing can be done. Finally you will get new alloy wheel.

    To increase the life time of alloy wheel paint, clean the wheels regularly and from time to time add a coat of wax.
